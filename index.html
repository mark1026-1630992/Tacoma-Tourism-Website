American Indians, predominantly the Puyallup people, who lived in settlements on the delta.

You'll Like Tacoma

Tacoma has at least two well-known slogans to hang its hat on: The City of Destiny and You’ll Like Tacoma.
While the original “You’ll Like Tacoma” sign stood on the shores of Lake Union and 110 years later the new LED sign depicting a heart
made up of Tacoma's area code (253) still assures visitors that you'll like Tacoma.


The Tacoma Dome

Built in 1983, the Tacoma Dome is one of the largest wood domed structures in the world. It holds all sorts of
events from local highschool sport tournaments to faumous musicians and bands, to political ralleys.


Water Front

Housing the Museum of Glass and The TAM (tacoma art museum), the water fronts port of Tacoma marine cargo operations is among
the largest in the United States, and was merged with the Port of Seattle's in 2015 to form the Northwest Seaport Alliance.


University of Washington, Tacoma

The University of Washingtons satellite campus was founded in 1997 and since then has grown five times in size.
UW Tacoma is an urban-serving university, closely linked with Tacoma and the South Puget Sound. The 46-acre campus footprint is
located on a hillside overlooking the Port of Tacoma and Mount Rainier, on the southern edge of downtown Tacoma,
next to museums and the beautifully reconstructed Union Station.
